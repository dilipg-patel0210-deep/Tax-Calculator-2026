Regime Max Rebate TI Limit Eligible?

HUF (any regime)——❌ NOT eligible — Sec 87A is for Individuals only
NRI (Non-Resident Indian)——❌ NOT eligible — Confirmed by Sec 87A text + multiple CAs
Why NRI cannot claim 87A: Section 87A of the Income Tax Act, 1961 explicitly states: "An assessee, being an individual resident in India..." — the word "resident" is a hard legislative condition. NRIs are non-residents under Sec 6 of the Act. Even if an NRI earns income in India and files an ITR, the rebate is not available. This is confirmed by ClearTax, Tax2Win, Bajaj Finserv, CA Club India, and ITR utility behavior.

RNOR is eligible: RNOR (Resident but Not Ordinarily Resident) is legally a resident under Sec 6. The "ordinarily resident" condition (Sec 6(6)) only affects taxability of foreign income — it does NOT affect the residency status for 87A. Therefore RNOR individuals get 87A rebate ✓.

Sec 87A applies ONLY on slab tax (FA 2025): Rebate CANNOT offset STCG 111A, LTCG 112A/112, Special rate 115BB/115BBH, or NRI flat rate 115A — even if Total Income ≤ threshold. This overrides ITAT Rajkot verdict (applicable AY 2024-25 only).
Marginal Relief (New Regime): TI ₹12L–₹12.75L → tax capped at (TI − ₹12L). Auto-applied.

🌾 Schedule EI: Agricultural Income u/s 10(1) · Sec 2(1A) i
Agricultural Income — u/s 10(1):
Fully Exempt: Agricultural income is exempt from income tax u/s 10(1).
Up to ₹5,000: Ignored entirely — no effect on tax. (NRI/HUF: always ignored.)
Above ₹5,000 (Old Regime · ROR/RNOR Individuals): Partial Integration Method applies:
Step 1: Add agri income to normal taxable income → compute tax on combined amount.
Step 2: Add agri income to basic exemption → compute tax on (agri + basic exemption).
Step 3: Net tax on normal income = Step1 tax − Step2 tax.
This method pushes normal income into higher slabs — effectively charging slab rate on regular income as if agri income were also taxed, but agri income itself is never actually taxed.
New Regime: Partial integration NOT applicable. Agri income exempt, no slab effect.
NRI: Agricultural income in India is exempt. No partial integration.
HUF: Partial integration applies same as Individual (Old Regime, if agri > ₹5,000).
▼
What qualifies as Agricultural Income u/s 2(1A):
✅ Rent/revenue from agricultural land in India (used for agriculture)
✅ Income from cultivation of agricultural produce (paddy, wheat, sugarcane, fruits, vegetables etc.)
✅ Income from farm buildings used for agricultural operations (situated on/near agricultural land)
✅ Income from saplings/seedlings grown in nursery
❌ Does NOT include: Poultry/dairy income, income from land used for non-agricultural purposes, dividend from tea/rubber/coffee companies (only 40–60% is agricultural)
